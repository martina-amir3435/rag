INTRODUCTION
I hope you enjoy this selection of healthy recipes which I have put together.
The recipes are just a few which I am sharing in my collection, there are
plenty more that you can discover on my website “Cooking With Emily.”
A few years ago, I needed to remove just over 3 stone (42 lbs) for health
reasons. And whilst my weight loss was about good exercise (trail running) I
also needed to be more mindful of how I ate and how to fuel my body.
BEFORE & AFTER
It wasn’t easy. Fully menopausal aged 50, and also emotionally not in a
good space, I needed to get a grip. This was more than vanity; it was so I
could live a healthier life without medications and interventions. I quickly
learnt what worked for me. I had to push on the exercise, but I also needed
food that made me feel full and gave me energy.
5
The weight came off within a few months, but what gave me greater joy
was that I started to feel so much fitter. I went from not being able to run
down the road, to doing Ultra Marathons (45Km) in the hills of the Lake
District.
Food was a major part. I cook, write recipes for a living, and so naturally
enjoy the naughty foods that I also produce. However, I now have a diet
that is 85% good food and can enjoy the 15% treat element, without
causing weight gain. For over 4 years, I have kept the weight off and
although I do my running and training, I make sure that I eat as much good
wholesome food as possible.
The one thing that helped me was having a few food dishes to hand. We all
get those days when we need some inspiration of what to cook or put
together to eat. This is that guide. As I have said, there are many more on
my website (some of which have video) that you can reference. By all
means, if there is an ingredient you are not keen of, then swap it for another
that you do like, as long as its a healthy swap. This healthy recipe pdf is to
help give you a guide.
Please ensure that before you start any new regime that you take advice
from your doctor. This is important for many groups including diabetics.
Also keep to your medications and again stick to GP advice.
One question I always get asked is about intermittent fasting and helping
hunger pangs. I intermittent fast until 1pm most days. However, when I am
doing a long run, then I will fuel up before I go with a decent breakfast. I
would stop eating at 7pm and not eat until 7am. Gradually over time I was
able to extend this to 1pm.
Eating, snacking and alcohol at night stopped, unless there was a special
occasion. My body needed to stop digesting food and get a break.
6
Fibre was my friend against hunger pangs. Fibre swells up and makes you
feel fuller for longer. I didn’t get overly hung up with protein, but did add
more into my diet than before. I also got a grip of my portion control. That
was way out of hand. Most recipes are for one, but many of the main
meals are for two people.
My mental outlook over it all is better too. Stop thinking diet, and think of a
new regime where you feel the benefits of increased health. I remember
the day I changed my lifestyle. It was just after Easter, where I was told by
a consultant to reduce my weight by 3 stone. I sat and ate my chocolate
egg and sobbed. I went on my first run and removed all bad influence
food in my home. However that day, all I could do was feel sorry for myself
and hold a pity party.
The difference to actually succeeding this time with weight loss and fitness,
was consistency. I kept diaries and logs of my exercise and food. I could
see weight coming off, my skin looking better, I had more energy and was
running further than I did as a teenager! These diaries helped me no end. I
could see how far I had come and that was a massive motivation to me.
So my final words to you are, is that do it gradually. Do it not just because
you will look better, but because your insides will be delighted that your
body will work better.
Oh and take no notice of other people and their opinions. If I had listened
to their sarcastic remarks, I would not be still running and living healthy and
well almost 5 years on! I think some were hoping I would fail. Live your life
for you. Do it for you. Stay in your own lane, and most of all do not go
comparing yourself to others. Remember you are unique, there is only one
of you.
You got this!
Emily x

BREAKFAST IDEAS
These are some breakfast ideas. Being totally honest, as I mentioned in the
introduction, I tend to skip breakfast as I intermittent fast most days. The
exception to this, is where I am away on holiday or if I am doing one of my
longer runs.
Some of these ideas I move to lunch. One of my favourites being avocado
and poached egg on whole grain or sourdough toast.
Whatever you do, do not cave into breakfast cereal. Even the so called
healthier ones are laden full of nasties. You will also note that I never use
low cal, low fat or any substitute for real food such as butter. For the most
part they taste nasty and second, there is more research coming out to say
that they are not that great for health.
I lost my weight with full fat proper food. A lot of it comes down to portion
control, discipline and moderation.

This simple, energising breakfast is perfect for those who want a quick, satisfying meal. The creamy
avocado combined with a perfectly poached egg offers healthy fats, protein, and plenty of energy to
start your day. With whole grain toast for fibre, this breakfast will keep you full and fuelled through the
morning.
Toast the slice of whole grain bread until golden and crispy.
While the bread is toasting, bring a small pot of water to a gentle
simmer. Add a dash of vinegar (optional) to help the egg whites set.
Crack the egg into a small bowl and carefully slide it into the simmering
water. Poach for 3-4 minutes for a soft, runny yolk.
While the egg is cooking, cut the avocado in half, remove the pit, and
scoop the flesh into a small bowl. Mash with a fork and season with salt
and pepper to taste.

"The difference between try and triumph is just a little 'umph'."
These Homemade Granola Bars with Oats and Honey are a nutritious, portable breakfast option or mid-
morning snack. Made with rolled oats, honey, and a handful of nuts and dried fruits, these bars provide
slow-releasing energy to fuel your day. Perfect for meal prep and completely customisable to suit your
taste.
Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F). Line a 20 cm x 20 cm (8 in x 8 in)
baking dish with parchment paper.
In a saucepan, melt the butter over low heat. Stir in the honey, vanilla
extract, cinnamon, and salt. Remove from heat once smooth.
In a large bowl, combine the oats, almonds, and dried cranberries. Pour
the wet mixture over the dry ingredients and mix thoroughly.
Transfer the mixture to the prepared baking dish and press firmly into an
even layer. Bake for 15-20 minutes until golden brown.
Allow the granola to cool completely in the pan before slicing into bars.
Store in an airtight container.

LUNCH IDEAS
Lunch was where it often went wrong in my previous attempts with weight
loss. Temptation got the better of me. Sandwiches, crisps, chocolate bar,
latte and juice. It was surprising how much I was eating, and most of it was
what I call “hollow calories.” To me, a lot of this food has calories but it
does not fill you up and keep you going. Salads became an obvious choice
for me, and I have tried not to put too many of them in this booklet.
However even now a salad has the ability to fill me up if there is plenty of it,
with different textures and variety. A limp bit of lettuce, tomato and
cucumber just isn’t going to cut it with me. So I hope some of the
suggestions here will help.

packed with protein. The earthy mushrooms and fresh spinach pair beautifully with the savoury ham,

6 CHERRY TOMATOES, HALVED
30 G (¼ CUP) BLACK OLIVES, PITTED AND

"Healthy eating is a form of self-respect."
This Tuna Niçoise Salad is a classic, protein-packed dish that’s perfect for a light yet satisfying lunch.
Made with tender new potatoes, crisp green beans, and hard-boiled eggs, it’s elevated by the bold
flavour of tuna and a zesty vinaigrette. Perfect for meal prep or a quick, healthy meal.
Place the new potatoes in a saucepan of salted boiling water. Cook for
10-12 minutes until tender. Drain and set aside to cool.
In the same pot, bring fresh water to a boil. Add the green beans and
cook for 2-3 minutes until tender-crisp. Drain and rinse under cold water
to stop the cooking process.
In a separate saucepan, place the eggs in boiling water and cook for 8-10
minutes. Cool under running cold water, peel, and cut into halves.
On a large plate or bowl, arrange the mixed greens as a base. Top with
potatoes, green beans, tuna, cherry tomatoes, olives, and egg halves.
Whisk together the olive oil, white wine vinegar, Dijon mustard, salt, and
pepper in a small bowl. Drizzle the dressing over the salad and serve
immediately.
APPROXIMATE NUTRITIONAL INFORMATION (PER SERVING)

Preheat your oven to 180°C (350°F).
Slice the sweet potato (or any other vegetable you’re using) thinly using a mandolin or sharp
knife.
Toss the vegetable slices in olive oil and season with the salt and rosemary.
Lay the slices out in a single layer on a baking sheet.
Bake for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway through, until they are crispy and golden.
Allow to cool before serving.

"Work out because you love your body, not because you hate it."
These protein-packed roll-ups are simple to make and can curb hunger quickly. The combination of lean
turkey and cheese provides a satisfying balance of protein and fats.
Lay the slices of turkey breast flat on a cutting board or plate.
Lay a lettuce leaf in.
Place a slice of cheese on top of each turkey slice.
Roll up the turkey and cheese into a tight roll. Then slice into sections.
Repeat with the remaining slices.
Serve immediately or keep chilled for a snack on the go.

Zucchini Fries are a great healthy alternative to regular fries. They're low in calories, packed with fibre,
and full of flavour. These crispy, oven-baked zucchini fries are perfect for snacking without the guilt.
Preheat your oven to 200°C (400°F) and line a baking tray with parchment paper.
In a shallow bowl, combine the breadcrumbs, parmesan cheese, garlic powder, oregano,
salt, and pepper.
Dip each zucchini fry into the beaten egg, then coat with the breadcrumb mixture. Place
the coated zucchini fries on the prepared baking tray.
Bake for 20–25 minutes, flipping halfway through, until the fries are golden and crispy.
Serve immediately with your favourite dipping sauce.... healthy of course!
